Introduction

Although more than 50 years have passed since the central dogma was advocated

and the dramatic progresses in DNA and protein technologies have been made, we still cannot produce a cell.

In the emerging research field of synthetic biology, how to create an artificial cell is becoming a hot topic, since it has been studied from 1990 [1].

A simple idea to mimic a cell is to produce a chemical vesicle, which is a small closure-sphere structure carrying the liquid phase in it.

The vesicles constructed by phospholipid are known as liposomes, which are considered as similar to the membrane structure in living cells.

Furthermore, the biochemical reactions inside the vesicles are further studied, for example, poly A synthesis [2, 3], GFP synthesis [4], and DNA replication [5].

These studies largely suggested that it is possible to mimic a cell in the test tubes.

In addition, an important system for mimicking the central dogma is the so-called cell-free translation system.

Among the cell-free translation systems, the PURE system is the only one that comprised purified components.

PURE system is omission of “Protein synthesis Using Recombinant Element system”, which comprises the essential components for transcription and translation [6, 7].

PURE system is different from the crude cell extract translation systems in terms of reconstruction of various factors,

thus it is a complete chemical system containing the purified components.

Using this system can help us to create an artificial cell using all purified proteins and chemicals.

In this study, we aim to create the artificial cells and try toregulate the gene expression in the artificial cells.

We use liposome as the membrane structure and the PURE system to obey the central dogma inside the cell.
